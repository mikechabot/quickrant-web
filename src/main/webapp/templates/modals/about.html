About

Quickrant was inspired by Chris Poole's notion of prismatic identity. If you check out the video, you'll note that he states people are fundamentally composed of multiple identities - that we're a different person to different people, and that social media should be built to reflect this.

The state of social media today is an interesting one, Facebook or otherwise; at times it can be considered a faux pas to express yourself in an impassioned or opinionated way, be it regarding a political, religious, or social thought - or even over something very deeply personal. As a result, Facebook can never be a perfect representation of your true self, nor any site tied to your identity, even as much as they purport themselves to be.

  • Who you are on social media is not who you are in life.
  • Who you are to your work or school is not who you are to your friends and family.
  • Who you are to your friends and family is not who you are when you are alone.
You should be able to express yourself without fear of having that expression harm a perception of you. Therein lies the purpose of quickrant: to give you the freedom to express yourself without being tied down to an identity, to say anything to everyone, and with no login required, ever.

Note: The only information I gather from you is used to protect my servers from you, and potential cyber-attacks you may mount. No information will ever be provided to a third-party, or governmental agency.
Express yourself without yourself. Say anything to everyone. No login required.
