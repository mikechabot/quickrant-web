About

Quickrant was inspired by Chris Poole's notion of prismatic identity, which states that a peron is composed of not one, but many identities through which they're perceived by the people around them -and that social media should be built to reflect this.

On Facebook and other social media sites, it's considered a faux pas to express yourself in an overly emotional or impassioned way, be it regarding a political, religious, or social view - or even over something very deeply personal. As a result, Facebook can never be a perfect representation of your true self, even as much as it purports itself to be.

  • Who you are on Facebook is not who you are in life.
  • Who you are to your work or school is not who you are to your friends and family.
  • Who you are to your friends and family is not who you are when you are alone.
You should be able to express yourself without fear of having that expression harm a perception of you. This is why I created quickrant: to give you the freedom to express yourself without being tied down to an identity, to say anything to everyone, and with no login required, ever.

To put it simply, what you say on quickrant can never be associated back to your person; you are free to say anything you'd like without fear of reprisal or consequence. Words are powerful tools, use them wisely.

Note: The only information I gather from you is used to protect my servers from you, and potential cyber-attacks you may mount. No information will ever be provided to a third-party, or governmental agency.
Express yourself without yourself. Say anything to everyone. No login required.
