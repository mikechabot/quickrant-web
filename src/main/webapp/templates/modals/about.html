About

Quickrant was inspired by Chris Poole's notion of "prismatic identity," which states a person is composed of numerous identities or personalities. We are not static beings, but rather a beautiful array of conflicting ideas and emotions:

  • Who you are on Facebook or Twitter is not who you are in life.
  • Who you are in the office is not who you are to your friends and family.
  • Who you are to your friends and family is not who you are when you are alone.
One should be able to express oneself without fear of having that expression harm the world's perception of them. This is why I created quickrant: to give people the freedom to express themselves without themselves. To say anything to everyone. And with no login required, ever.

To put it simply, what you say on quickrant can never be associated back to your person; you are free to say anything you'd like without fear of reprisal or consequences.

Note: The only information I gather from you is used to protect myself from you, and potential cyber-attacks you may mount. No information will ever be provided to a third-party, or governmental agency.
